AUTONOMOUS SYSTEMS & FORCE ARCHITECTURE

Unmanned systems fundamentally alter battlefield dynamics by extending operational reach while minimizing personnel exposure. The proliferation of affordable drones has dramatically lowered barriers to air operations: "conduct most if not all of the airpower roles for the price of a drone, a laptop, and some imagination."

Commercial supply chains now provide essential components — batteries, computing systems, airframe materials — accessible through standard e-commerce platforms.

Ukrainian drones have destroyed over 65 percent of destroyed Russian tanks, demonstrating significant economic disruption. Low-cost attritable systems achieve outsized effects when deployed at scale.

The conflict revealed a four-tier military asset taxonomy:
- Expendable assets (single-use ammunition)
- Attritable assets (low-cost, replaceable systems)
- Risk-tolerant assets (medium-tier unmanned systems)
- Survivable assets (high-value platforms requiring protection)

Drones serve multiple functions: reconnaissance, targeting, strike operations, electronic warfare platforms, communications relays, and logistics support.

Drone operators became high-value targets. Both sides developed countermeasures including mesh networks, satellite communications integration, airborne relays, and local autonomy capabilities.

ELECTRONIC WARFARE & SPECTRUM SUPERIORITY

After three decades of Western electromagnetic dominance in Iraq and Afghanistan, Ukraine revealed a genuinely contested electromagnetic landscape where neither side achieved clear advantage.

GPS jamming disrupted navigation for autonomous systems and precision-guided munitions. Communications disruption degraded command and control. Tactical jamming targeted UAV operations. Frequency agility created temporary operational windows.

Emerging requirements: modern agile software-defined systems; integrated military-government-commercial domain architectures; AI integration enabling cognitive electronic warfare at machine speed.

AIR DEFENSE EVOLUTION

Modern air defense confronts diverse threats from low-cost one-way attack drones to high-end hypersonic missiles.

Ukraine implemented extensive acoustic sensor networks creating comprehensive air pictures; diversified beyond traditional radar systems; and integrated multiple sensing modalities for resilient detection networks.

The concept of air superiority now encompasses electromagnetic spectrum control, integrated kinetic and non-kinetic effects, and autonomous system employment.

CONTESTED LOGISTICS

Western militaries systematically reduced organic logistics capabilities over 30-40 years. The emerging approach emphasizes "disaggregate to survive, reaggregate when necessary" with distributed supply nodes, rapid concentration capability, and additive manufacturing reducing supply chain dependency.

KEY CONCLUSION: Military effectiveness increasingly depends on continuous testing, experimentation, and validation rather than seeking perfect end states. "Better never stops."
