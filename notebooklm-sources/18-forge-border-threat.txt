# US-Mexico Border Drone Threat & Counter-Drone Opportunities

## 1. Scale of Cartel Drone Operations

- **34,682 drone flights** detected within 500m of border in FY2025 (CBP)
- **60,000 flights** by 27,000 unique craft in just Jul-Dec 2024
- **~1,000 drones/month** crossing from Mexico into US
- **221 weaponized drone incidents** in Mexico 2021-2025, **77 fatalities**
- **120+ cartel drone attacks** in Mexico in 2025 alone
- Michoacan and Guerrero hottest zones (55-80 incidents in 2025)

### Drones Used
- Consumer: DJI Avata 2 (~$600, CJNG)
- FPV drones adopted from Ukrainian tactics
- Custom heavy-lift: up to 100 kg payload, 45+ min flight, 100+ mph
- Emerging: fiber-optic drones (RF-jamming resistant)

### Tactics
- **Reconnaissance**: Monitor Border Patrol and military positions, guide smuggling
- **Drug smuggling**: Fentanyl, meth (CBP seized drone with 3.6 lbs fentanyl pills)
- **Weaponized attacks**: IEDs with nails/metal fragments (Oct 2025: 3 drones hit Baja California prosecutor's office near California border)
- **Prison contraband**: Drugs and cell phones
- **Operating hours**: Primarily 8 PM - 4 AM

### Organization
- CJNG has dedicated **"Drone Operators" unit** — 42 incidents, 21 deaths
- Sinaloa Cartel adopted FPVs by September 2025
- Cartel operatives may have infiltrated Ukraine's International Legion for FPV training (Defense News, July 2025)

## 2. Counter-Drone Systems Currently Deployed

### DHS / CBP
- **SkySafe** cloud-based drone detection (DHS S&T / CBP contract)
- **Citadel Defense Titan** C-UAS ($1M+ contract)
- **Anti-drone laser** (military-provided) deployed near Fort Bliss Feb 2026

### DoD / NORTHCOM
- **JTF-Southern Border** established March 2025, HQ Fort Huachuca AZ, ~10,000 troops
- **Lockheed Martin AN/TPQ-53** multi-mission radar — 360-degree surveillance
- **Anduril C-UAS kits** to USNORTHCOM:
  - Mobile Sentry (autonomous detection/tracking)
  - Wisp/SkyFence (wide-area passive IR)
  - Pulsar (RF detection + EW)
  - Anvil (autonomous kinetic interceptor)
  - All on Lattice C2
- **JIATF 401** established August 2025 — rapidly integrate/test/deliver C-UAS capabilities

### DHS Counter-Drone Office (Jan 2026)
- New permanent Program Executive Office for UAS and Counter-UAS
- **$115 million** initial investment

## 4. Legal Framework

### Who Can Take Down Drones
- **Only DoD, DOE, DOJ, DHS** have statutory authority
- All others: taking down a drone is **illegal** (Aircraft Sabotage Act + Communications Act of 1934)
- RF jamming additionally restricted by FCC

### Detection vs. Mitigation — THE KEY DISTINCTION
- **Detection and tracking: broadly permissible.** No license needed for passive sensing.
- **Active interference: tightly restricted** to authorized federal entities.
- This is the most important legal fact for a startup.

### SAFER SKIES Act (Dec 18, 2025) — Game Changer
- First federal framework allowing trained state/local law enforcement to detect, track, seize, disable, or destroy drones posing "credible threat"
- Officers must complete federal training, use approved systems
- Technologies must come from **federally-approved list** (early approval = competitive moat)
- Authorities extended through **2031**
- Covers: correctional facilities, sporting events, critical infrastructure, military installations

### Arizona State Law (2025)
- Immunity for peace officers shooting down drones within 15 miles of border if suspected of smuggling

## 5. CBP Procurement Process

- SAM.gov is central portal (opportunities over $25K)
- CBP Small Business Program: monthly meetings via DHS OSDBU
- **DHS SBIR**: Quantum Technology Sciences went SBIR -> $10M Phase III with CBP
- Blanket Purchase Agreements (BPAs) designed for small businesses
- Unsolicited proposals accepted
- DHS S&T acts as innovation funnel -> transitions to operational components
- **$1.5B C-UAS IDIQ** designed for rapid procurement

**Key difference from DoD:** DHS/CBP moves faster for COTS technology, lower barriers via SBIR/BPA.

## 6. State and Local Law Enforcement

### Cochise County, AZ (84 miles of border)
- Draganfly drone program: Outrider prototypes with thermal, GPS, AI, 7-hour flight
- Drones designed to cope with cartel jamming

### Broader Situation
- Border sheriffs in escalating "drone arms race" with cartels
- Primary need: **detection, tracking, alerting** — forward intelligence to federal agencies
- SAFER SKIES Act + $500M FEMA grants will drive state/local procurement wave in 2026-2027
- **Massive underserved market**: hundreds of agencies along 1,954 miles with zero counter-drone capability

## 7. The Detection-Only Advantage

- Detection operates within existing legal frameworks **without restriction**
- No license, no federal authorization needed
- $500M FEMA C-UAS Grant scope explicitly includes "detect, identify, track, or monitor"
- SAFER SKIES Act requires technologies on federally-approved list — **early approval = moat**

### Detection Market Size
- **$858.6M in 2025** -> **$2.8B by 2030** -> **$8.4B by 2034** (28.9% CAGR)

## 8. Incidents Where Better Detection Would Have Helped

- **Feb 2026 El Paso**: Military laser shot at **party balloons**, shutting down El Paso International Airport. Better classification would have prevented it.
- **Feb 2026 CBP drone shootdown**: Military shot down a CBP drone because CBP flew it into military airspace without notice. IFF/detection integration would have prevented friendly fire.
- **34,682 detected flights**: Many more undetected. CSIS: "next to impossible" to cover full 3,100 km with current capabilities.
- **Oct 2025 Tijuana bombing**: 3 explosive drones hit prosecutor's office 1 mile from California border. Cross-border detection = early warning.
- **Nightly smuggling**: Drones operating 8 PM - 4 AM provide cartel ISR. Persistent detection enables interdiction of ground operations.

## 10. Military Bases with Drone Incursions

- **Fort Bliss, TX**: Epicenter. ~10,000 troops. Two laser incidents Feb 2026. Disrupted El Paso airport.
- **Fort Huachuca, AZ**: JTF-SB headquarters. Near border.
- **Camp Pendleton, CA**: 6 confirmed incursions Dec 9-15, 2024.
- **NORTHCOM**: 350+ unauthorized incursions across 100 installations in 2024.
- Pentagon broadened counter-drone authorities Jan 2026 for base defense.

## Strategic Takeaways

1. **Detection-only is lowest-friction entry.** No legal barriers, eligible for $500M FEMA grants, prerequisite capability everyone needs.
2. **SAFER SKIES Act creates new market.** State/local agencies authorized for first time, but only with approved tech. Early approval = moat.
3. **SBIR-to-Phase III works.** Quantum Technology Sciences: SBIR -> $10M CBP contract.
4. **$1.5B DHS C-UAS IDIQ** is the target vehicle. Mandatory across all DHS components.
5. **Multi-sensor fusion with AI classification** addresses worst operational failures (shooting balloons, friendly fire).
6. **Fiber-optic drones are the emerging gap.** RF detection going blind. Acoustic/visual become critical.
