SOURCE: Center for Strategic and International Studies (CSIS)
TITLE: The United States Needs a Southwest Drone Wall
AUTHOR: Henry Ziemer, Associate Fellow, Americas Program
DATE: December 16, 2025
URL: https://www.csis.org/analysis/united-states-needs-southwest-drone-wall

---

The article highlights that approximately 1,000 drone incursions occur monthly across the U.S.-Mexico border. Border patrol agents report being "watched every day" by unidentified drones hovering just across the Rio Grande.

Criminal organizations employ unmanned aerial systems to track border patrol movements, monitor U.S. military forces, deliver narcotics and contraband, and scout new smuggling routes. A recent explosive-armed drone attack on a Mexican police station in Tijuana demonstrates the escalating threat.

Cartels are experimenting with fiber-optic controlled drones — technology "resilient to jamming and other electronic warfare attacks." They're also testing larger platforms like the DJI FlyCart 30, capable of transporting payloads exceeding 80 pounds.

RECOMMENDED SOLUTIONS:

The article advocates for "distributed, passive networks of acoustic, electro-optical, and infrared sensors" rather than traditional advanced radar systems. This approach offers greater coverage across 3,100+ kilometers of border, increased resilience through redundancy, and better detection of low-and-slow flying drones.

The Department of Defense should designate borderland military bases as test zones for emerging counter-UAS technologies. The 10th Mountain Division currently uses AN/TPQ-53 and AN/MPQ-64 radars for detection, while new jamming systems provide soft-kill options.

Joint Task Force-Southern Border should serve as backbone. Partnership between FAA, CBP, and local law enforcement. JIATF 401 to contribute counter-UAS expertise.

CRITICAL CHALLENGES:
- Achieving persistent surveillance across thousands of kilometers
- Managing false positives to prevent accidentally jamming commercial aircraft
- Cost disparity — countermeasures often exceed drone expenses
- Cross-border operational constraints
- Rapid discovery and exploitation of coverage gaps
- Constant evolution of cartel tactics

STRATEGIC IMPORTANCE:
Border drone defense serves as a crucial testbed for future high-intensity conflicts. The technological and interagency solutions developed here will prove essential for broader homeland defense priorities.
