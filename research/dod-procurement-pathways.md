# DoD Procurement Pathways for a New Small Business (March 2026)

## 1. SBIR/STTR Program Status

The SBIR/STTR program authorization **expired on October 1, 2025**, and as of March 2026, **it has not been reauthorized**. New solicitations are paused, though existing awards remain valid and funded.

Three competing legislative paths exist:

- **H.R. 5100** — A simple one-year extension through September 30, 2026, with no programmatic changes. Already passed the House.
- **S.1573 / H.R.3169 (SBIR/STTR Reauthorization Act of 2025)** — Would make the programs permanent and raise agency set-aside targets to 7% for SBIR and 1% for STTR over time.
- **INNOVATE Act** — Would create a simplified Phase I-A award for first-time applicants, new large follow-on awards, and potential caps on lifetime awards per company.

**Bottom line:** SBIR/STTR is currently unavailable for new solicitations. Do not build entry strategy around SBIR right now.

## 2. Other Transaction Authority (OTA)

OTAs are the **single best entry point** for a non-traditional company selling hardware. Exempt from most FAR requirements — no cost accounting standards, simplified IP terms, faster timelines.

**How it works:** The government executes agreements with organized consortia. When a DoD customer posts a requirement, consortium members submit white papers (typically 5-10 pages), and winners get prototype OT agreements, often in 60-90 days.

**Key consortia to join:**

| Consortium | Manager | Focus Area | Annual Dues |
|---|---|---|---|
| **NSTXL** (multiple) | NSTXL | Broad defense tech, SOF | ~$250-$10,000 |
| **S2MARTS** | NSTXL | Microelectronics, strategic missions | Included in NSTXL |
| **SOSSEC** (8 OTAs) | SOSSEC Inc. | C4ISR, propulsion, cyber, supply chain | $500/year |
| **DIBC** | ATI | Broad DoD prototyping | Varies |

**Critical fact:** Only small businesses and non-traditional defense contractors (NDCs) have unrestricted access to OTA awards. A company with no prior DoD contracts qualifies as an NDC by definition.

## 3. Defense Innovation Unit (DIU)

DIU uses **Commercial Solutions Openings (CSOs)** — any company, including first-timers, can respond.

**Process:**
1. DIU posts solicitations on diu.mil/work-with-us
2. Submit a short solution brief
3. If interested, DIU schedules a pitch
4. Winners get OT prototype contracts, typically 12-24 months
5. Successful prototypes transition to production contracts

**Current active areas (early 2026):**
- **Counter-drone sensing** — DIU launched a new C-UAS sensing initiative in February 2026, Phase 2 demos at Yuma Proving Ground spring 2026
- **Containerized Autonomous Drone Delivery Systems (CADDS)**
- **Counter-UAS interceptors** — Follow-on from Replicator 2
- **Low-cost sensing** — MatrixSpace won the C-UAS low-cost sensing challenge in December 2025

## 4. AFWERX, Army xTech, NavalX

### AFWERX (Air Force)
- New SBIR activity **paused** due to authorization lapse
- Non-SBIR mechanisms remain active: **STRATFI** ($3M-$15M), **TACFI**
- STRATFI/TACFI require existing SBIR history or private investment — harder for brand-new company

### Army xTech
- **Active.** Nearly 40 competitions since 2018, continuing in 2025-2026
- Prize competitions: non-dilutive cash prizes, mentorship, networking
- No prior contracts needed. Watch xtechsearch.army.mil

### NavalX
- Facilitation layer, not direct contracting
- Tech Bridges: regional innovation hubs connecting Navy/Marine Corps needs with industry

## 5. APFIT

Over **$1 billion awarded** to small businesses since launching in 2022.

- Award size: $10M-$50M per project
- Purpose: Procurement funding for technologies that have **completed development**
- **You cannot submit directly** — a DoD organization must nominate/sponsor your project
- This is a later-stage play after proving your product works

## 6. Direct Commercial Procurement

As of October 1, 2025:

| Threshold | Amount | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| **Micro-Purchase** | **$15,000** | Purchase card, no competitive bidding, minimal paperwork |
| **Simplified Acquisition** | **$350,000** | Simplified procedures, set aside for small businesses |

**If your hardware unit costs under $15,000, a DoD unit commander can buy it on a purchase card today with almost no friction.**

## 7. Border Security / Counter-Drone Procurement

### DHS Counter-Drone Office (January 2026)
- New permanent Program Executive Office for UAS and Counter-UAS
- **$115 million** immediate investment
- **$1.5 billion contract vehicle** — solicitation open/upcoming
- **$250 million** in counter-UAS grants to FIFA World Cup host states

### CBP Border Operations
- **FY2026 technology budget: up to $1.5 billion** for drones and counter-drone
- Accelerating purchases of human-portable drones for single-agent launch

### DoD at the Border
- Pentagon deploying counter-drone capabilities at US-Mexico border
- **DIU and NORTHCOM** partnered specifically for counter-drone challenges at the border
- DIU February 2026 counter-drone sensing initiative covers "homeland and mobile defense"

## 8. What a New Company Needs

### Mandatory (Free)
1. **Unique Entity ID (UEI)** — Through SAM.gov. Free.
2. **SAM.gov Registration** — Free. Takes 7-10 business days to 3-5 weeks.
3. **CAGE Code** — Assigned automatically with SAM registration.
4. **NAICS Codes** — Selected during SAM registration.

### Strongly Recommended
5. **Small Business Certifications** — Confirm size standard with SBA.
6. **OTA Consortium Memberships** — NSTXL (~$250-$10,000) and SOSSEC ($500).
7. **GSA Schedule** (later) — 3-6 months to obtain.

### For Classified Work (Future)
8. **Facility Clearance** — Cannot self-sponsor; needs government/contractor sponsor.
9. **CMMC Certification** — Level 1 (self-assessment) for basic; Level 2 for CUI.

## Recommended Entry Strategy

1. **Register on SAM.gov** immediately (free, 1-3 weeks)
2. **Join NSTXL and SOSSEC** (under $1,000, gives OTA access)
3. **Monitor DIU** — especially counter-drone CSOs
4. **Enter Army xTech competitions** for visibility and non-dilutive funding
5. **If unit cost < $15,000**, market directly to operational units (purchase card buys)
6. **Watch for DHS $1.5B counter-drone contract vehicle**
7. **Do not wait for SBIR** — use OTA, DIU, xTech instead
8. **Build a DoD champion** inside a program office for eventual APFIT nomination

## Timeline to First Dollar
- Week 1-3: SAM.gov registration, UEI, CAGE code
- Week 2-4: Join OTA consortia, monitor DIU
- Month 1-3: Submit first white papers
- Month 3-6: Potential first prototype OT award (60-90 days)
- Month 6+: GSA Schedule, larger opportunities
