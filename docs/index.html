Why Acoustic Detection Is the Wedge

The Fiber-Optic Drone Problem

Fiber-optic FPV drones use a thin cable (0.1-0.3mm) for video/control. This completely eliminates the RF command link. Russia has deployed these at scale since spring 2024. NATO issued an urgent Innovation Challenge in April 2025 specifically for this problem.

What breaks: RF detection, RF jamming, RF direction-finding — the majority of the C-UAS market.

What still works: Acoustic detection (motors still make sound — often louder due to cable weight), thermal/visual, radar (if RCS sufficient).

Acoustic Drone Signatures

Drone propellers produce a "harmonic stack" — fundamental blade passing frequency (BPF) plus integer multiples. Multi-rotors also exhibit rotor-rotor interaction tones (beat frequencies from mismatched RPM) that strongly discriminate from birds/aircraft.

Key Lessons

  1. Cheap drones changed everything — treated like ammunition
  2. Counter-drone is the urgent unsolved problem
  3. EW/jamming is necessary but insufficient (fiber-optic immune)
  4. Edge AI and offline operation are essential (networks get denied)
  5. Multi-layered defense-in-depth is the only approach that works
  6. The side that adapts fastest wins

Startup Formation & Compliance

Entity: Delaware C-Corp

VCs require it. Stripe Atlas or Clerky ($500-800). Registered agent ($100-300/yr). Total under $1,500.

ITAR/EAR

Detection-only = likely EAR (Commerce). Much lighter than ITAR. Passive RF scanning, acoustic, optical — reasonable argument for EAR99 jurisdiction. If you add jamming/defeat capabilities, that's ITAR (State Dept). Stay detection-only initially.

CMMC

Level 1 (self-assessment): ~$3-5K. Level 2 (third-party, for CUI): $50K-200K first year. You don't need it before your first contract. Build compliant habits from day one.

Security Clearance

Not needed initially. Counter-drone detection at unclassified level doesn't require clearance. Pursue FCL later when a specific program demands it.

Key Standards (Later, Not Now)

  • MIL-STD-810H (ruggedization): Self-declared after testing. $10-50K.
  • MIL-STD-461G (EMI/EMC): Critical for RF devices. $15-40K.
  • Many prototype contracts accept commercial-grade hardware.

The Valley of Death — How to Survive

  1. Dual-use from day one — prisons, airports, events = non-DoD revenue
  2. DIU pathway — 51% of prototypes transition to production
  3. Stay lean — don't staff up before production revenue
  4. Multiple contract vehicles — don't depend on one program
  5. Find your champion — operational end-users, not just acquisition
