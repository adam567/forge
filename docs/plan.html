FORGE — Business Plan

A plain-English explanation of what this is, why it works, and what it costs.

What Are We Building?

A small, rugged box that listens for drones and tells you when one is nearby. It uses microphones, a tiny computer with artificial intelligence, and a thermal camera. It works without internet. It runs on a battery with a solar panel. You can put it on a fence post, a rooftop, or in a Pelican case in a truck.

One box tells you "there's a drone coming from that direction." Three boxes spread across an area tell you exactly where the drone is.

Why Does Anyone Need This?

Drones are now weapons. Not someday — right now.

  • Ukraine: 200,000 military drones deployed per month. Drones have become the defining weapon of modern war.
  • US-Mexico border: 34,682 cartel drone flights detected near the border in 2025. Cartels use drones for drug smuggling, surveillance of Border Patrol, and weaponized attacks with explosives.
  • Prisons: 43% increase in drone incidents. Drones deliver drugs, phones, and weapons over prison walls.
  • Military bases: 350+ unauthorized drone incursions across 100 US military installations in 2024.

The US government is spending billions on counter-drone technology right now. This is not speculative.

Why Doesn't This Already Exist?

Most current drone detectors work by listening for the radio signals drones use to communicate with their pilot. The problem: the newest and most dangerous drones — fiber-optic drones and autonomous drones — don't use radio signals at all. They're invisible to existing detection systems.

But they still have motors and propellers. They still make sound. Our device listens for that sound and uses AI to recognize it.

Ukraine has deployed 14,000+ of these acoustic sensors at about $500 each, and they work — 95% interception support rate. But no one sells a commercial version of this in the United States.

That's the gap we fill.

What Happens If It Doesn't Work?

Worst case: we're out $10,000.

That's real money but not catastrophic. Here's why the downside is bounded:

  • No loans, no debt. All equity, all ours.
  • No employees, no payroll obligations.
  • No office lease. Build it at home.
  • The hardware has resale value — Jetson boards, SDRs, cameras, and Pelican cases all sell used.
  • The knowledge and SAM.gov registration are permanent assets even if this specific product doesn't fly.
  • I keep my day job throughout. No income gap.

Why Me?

  • No hardware background — but the hardware is commercial off-the-shelf components. The value isn't in designing chips. It's in system integration, software, compliance, and understanding the customer. Ukraine built 14,000 acoustic sensors using Android phones and satellite dishes.

Why Now?

Three things happened in the last 90 days that make this moment unique:

  1. SAFER SKIES Act (December 18, 2025) — For the first time in US history, state and local law enforcement can legally counter drones. This created thousands of new buyers overnight. Technologies must be on a federally-approved list. Early approval = competitive advantage.
  2. DHS Counter-Drone Office (January 12, 2026) — $115M immediate investment, $1.5B contract vehicle being solicited right now.
  3. DIU Counter-Drone Sensing Initiative (February 2026) — The Defense Innovation Unit is actively looking for exactly what we're building. Demos at Yuma Proving Ground this spring.

The money is allocated. The solicitations are open. The gap is documented. The question is whether we move fast enough to be in the room.
